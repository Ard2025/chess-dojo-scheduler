Coming Soon

The Dojo is moving into the final stretch of preparing for the launch of 3.0. Thereâs a lot to do! Not only do we have feedback from you, but we have a broad array of statistics we are using to rethink our assumptions about what produces the best results for each cohort. The Dojo will always continue to improve, but here are a couple things we are hoping for in Dojo 3.0, planned for May 1st.

Tactics Rating!  - Using stats, we have been able to identify some key tests of tactical strength. The main point of this rating is to tell players if they should be doing more tactics or if they need to move on to something else. It will hopefully also be fun, in a Dojo type two fun kind of way.

Revamping of Reqs  - We are adding many more materials to the program. In the past it was not uncommon to have the same material in the next band. 3.0 will change this.

New Rating Conversion Table - Even without the dreaded FIDE rating boost we wanted to re-do our rating table. This is a hard one. Not only is the rating system thrown into turmoil by the boost but the cohorts from 1000-1400 are particularly hard to gauge. A 1000 turned into a 1400, so who is now between 1000 and 1400?

Automatic Profile Review - When a Dojoer reaches 80% completion of their cohortâs training plan. The assumption of the training program from the beginning was that if someone does all the reqs they will make it to the next level (at least usually). We are therefore instituting an automatic review for those who are getting close.

Program Updates

Openings - We made a significant shift in how we score openings. Because it was hard to say when we are done with an opening and we wanted to encourage people to extend their post-mortems after sparring we are now going to begin awarding opening points based on the amount of time you spend. A video explainer is here.
